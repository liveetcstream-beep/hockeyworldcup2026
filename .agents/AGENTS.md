# Content Generation & Optimization Guidelines

When writing or modifying articles, pages, or previews for this website, always follow the custom event blogging content formula.

## 📝 Content Creation Formula
Every content piece must strictly adhere to the following rules:

1. **Semantic Entity Coverage**: Include all key semantic entities (official names, specific stadiums, coach names, player positions, exact dates, and TV/streaming rights holders).
2. **Inline Content Questions**: Structure sections around natural, user-intent search questions as subheadings (e.g., "Where to watch...", "Who has been named in the squad?", "When is kickoff?").
3. **Structured FAQ Block**: Always end the page or article with a detailed FAQ section using the `<FaqAccordion>` component or structured HTML format, matching exact search queries from People Also Ask (PAA).
4. **E-E-A-T Signals**: Integrate expert byline info boxes (e.g., "Verified by Marc Devos, Senior Hockey Journalist") to signal authority, trust, and accuracy to search engines.
5. **NLP Optimization**: Focus on natural, highly informative phrasing. Direct answers to queries should be provided within the first 100-150 words of a section.
6. **Spectator Pain Points**: Address real spectator needs (timezone conversions, local parking issues, ticket verification rules, streaming geoblocking updates).
7. **Human Written Tone**: Use an engaging, conversational, sports-expert tone. Absolutely avoid generic AI transition phrases, buzzwords, or predictable templates (e.g., avoid "in conclusion", "furthermore", "delve", "testament").
